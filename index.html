About Me

I’m a computer architecture enthusiast with a focus on CPU design, microarchitecture, and performance analysis. I work at Qualcomm in San Diego as a CPU architect and performance engineer, where I analyze bottlenecks, tune performance, and think about how hardware and software interact at a low level.
I enjoy methodical, engineering-oriented problem solving: building mental models, validating them with data, and iterating. That same mindset extends into my hobbies — from running a Proxmox-based homelab and self‑hosting services, to experimenting with networking, firewalls (OPNsense), and secure personal infrastructure.
Outside of tech, I love scuba diving, swimming, and exploring new places. I’m also drawn to philosophy and longer-term learning — I’d rather understand systems deeply than chase quick wins.
